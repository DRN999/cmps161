OverTracker(Overwatch data tracker)

For my final project I am planning a data visulaizer called OverTracker. This basic concept of this data tracker is to visualize data from overwatch player from masteroverwatch.com to analyze player data and compare them with others and tries to create a visualization that would benifit the player.

Overwatch:

Overwatch is a FPS(first-person shooter) game that was created/sold by Blizzard Entertainment. The game in standard mode is played with 6 players vs 6 players teams, in which each team tries to complete an objective depending on the map they are placed in for the particular game. Each player in the 6 people team are to select from 23 different playable characters(no 2 player in the same team can select the game character), and create a team composition to out play the opponent team.

The reason why I wanted to do this:

This might seem weird for people who know/use masteroverwatch because this website does include some visuals for the analysis of the data. However, as a player of this video game myself, I want more than that. The main issue I have with this data tracker is that it takes your overall data(average of all the data you accumilated so far) and visulalizes it. There is an issue with that, and it is the fact that you it only tells you how many games you lost and won in total and give you your average stats for all the games you played. I personally cannot benefit from this because I have no idea what I did right in the games that I won and what I did wrong in the games that I lost. I found this to be perfect oppurtunity for me to create a data visulalizer of my own that can not only visualize general data but can also input specific data per game and do a more in-depth analysis on my game play specifically.

My plan for this project:

I am currently thinking two methods of visualizing the data: General Visualization(for this I have 2 ideas) and Specific Visualization.

General Status Visualization:
This portion is to take the statistics taken from masteroverwatch and visualizing it.

First Plan:
My first plan is to make a 3D plot consisting of different components for each character for the axis. For example I am going to use this chracter called Ana in this game. She is a support character that can heal but also deal some significant amounts of damage to the enemy as well. She also has a special ability where she can shoot a projectile(that is relatively hard to hit) that will make one of the enemies sleep on impact, making the enemy hit by this "sleep dart" vulnurable to you and yur teammates. For her I can make a 3D plot point where X-axis is the damage done to the enemy per game, Y-axis is healing done per game, and Z-axis is the amount of sleep-darts hit per game. The point will also have a size representing the "Kill to death" ratio of the player represented by the size of the point. Bigger the point, the more the player gets a kill on the enemy team each time the player dies. The colors will be differenciated for each player when multiple player data are inputted.

Second Plan:
My second plan is to take all data of each character consisting of 13 plot points. The plot points will be organized in a way that will create a surface of revolution shape about the Y-axis creating a shape that looks like a pyramid with six edges on top of a hexagonal prism.

This will result in a 3D star-plot including these 13 data points:

  • Eliminations(kills)
  • Play Time
  • Kill to Death ratio
  • Weapon Accuracy
  • Damage done
  • Blocked Damage
  • Healing
  • Objective Kills
  • Ojbective Time
  • Critival Hits(head shots)
  • Offensive Assist(or scoped accuracy)
  • Defensive Assist(or unscoped accuracy)
  • Special stats for each character (Ex: Ana's sleep darts - sleeps darts hit %)

The user is given the option to slice this 3D shape with a plane. This plane will go through the 3D shape in a way that will create a clear cross secion going through each point of the shape. Then the visualizer will show the cross section of the model to show a 2D star plot showing the points that the plane intersected. The user may also rotate the plane in different angles to change the cross section in order to see a different star plot that involves different point of the original 3D star-plot.

Specific Status Visualization:
This side will involve more data taking than the previous part, because I need to get the data from my very own(or friend's) data. Collection of data can be easily done by using the screenshot feature already implemented in the game. I can use the screenshot of of the game ending screen(all the character data are shown at the end of the game) to take the data of how well I did that gameplay.
The main data collected by these session will include these(*all these are going to be raw number per game, not average number per game):

  • Eliminations(kills)
  • Damage Done
  • Healing Done
  • Character Special(Ex: Ana's sleep-dart accuracy)

The program will then start computing these data, resulting in separation of data. This examplanation is unclear so here is an example. Lets say we have data for 10 games that I played, where I won 5 games and lost 5 games. I look at how many eliminations I got in each of the games and put them into a ordered list(low to high) to compare them. Then I compare these data to separate them into 2 list: one list containing the games that I had relatively low eliminations, and the second list with the games that I generally had high number of eliminations. Then this data will be plot onto a 2D graph containing amount of wins as the X-axis, and the amount of lossed as the Y-axis. The larger list and the smaller list will be color-coded, and the size will represent how large the average is. This data visualization can give many information to the player. According to how I designed this graph, when the average of either of the data gets closer to the y = x line (the 45 degree diagonal line through the origin), your performance on the particular status does not affect your percentage to win or lose a game. And also, the closer the point is to the origin, it means that the player does not play in that manner consistently, because the number of win and losses are low in that category. On the other hand, the further away the point is from the y = x line, the more impact it has to your win to loss ratio. And this also means that the further away the point is from the origin, the player performs more consistently in the manner that the data show.
